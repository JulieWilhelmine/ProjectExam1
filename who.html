The very first!

Yuri Gagarin

First man in Space

Yuri, a Soviet pilot, was the first to complete an orbit of Earth in April 1961. The spacecraft, named Vostok, was rather small so it was good that Yuri was only 1.57 meters tall. Upon launch Yuri exclaimed “poyekhali!“, meaning “let's go!” which later became a historical phrase to describe the beginning of the Space Age in human history. In 1968 he died in a jet crash.

Valentina Tereshkova

First woman in Space

Valentina, a russian engineer and politician, was the first woman to travel to space, in 1963. She also became the first civillian to go to space, as everyone before her had been a part of the military. To this day she is the only woman to go on a solo space mission. It was her experience in skydiving – a hobby of hers, that led to her selection as a astronaut. She is still alive and well in Russia and says she would love to go to Mars, even if it's to be a one way trip.

Neil Armstrong

First man on the Moon

In July 1969 an American aeronautical engineer, naval aviator, test pilot, and university professor named Neil Armstrong became the first man to set foot on the Moon. It was he who said the famous words "That's one small step for (a) man, one giant leap for mankind." Neil was an eager Boy Scout and greeted the scouts when flying to the Moon, as well as carried a World Scout Badge with him there and back. He died after a surgery in 2012, 82 years old.

Buzz Aldrin

Second man on the Moon

The American engineer and Command Pilot in the United States Air Force, Buzz Aldrin, was the second man to set foot on the Moon, as a part of Neil Armstrong's crew in 1969. A religious man, Buzz consumed food and drink on the Moon, taking communion. His birth name was not Buzz, but when his sisters pronounced the word “brother” as “buzzer” he changed that to his legal name. Since 1985 he has worked on a special space flight path known as the Aldrin Cycler which uses less propellant than usual means. He is still working on it today, 88 years old.

Alexander Gerst

Currently in space!

The German European Space Agency astronaut and geophysicist Alexander returned to space in June of 2018 and is the current commander on the International Space Station – the youngest there has been at the age of 42. Home in Germany he has volunteered as a scout leader, firefighter, and water rescue lifeguard, as well as received a reward for outstanding research. For this current mission he brought with him a robot assistant named CIMON.

Andrew Feustel

Arrived at Earth Oct. 2018

With a Ph.D. in the Geological Sciences, specializing in Seismology, and a veteran of two spaceflights, American Andrew was the commander of Expedition 55 and 56. His most recent trip had him on the International Space Station from March to October, 2018. Singing and playing guitar he is a part of the astronaut band Max Q. He also enjoys car restoration and skiing.

Oleg Artemyev

Arrived at Earth Oct. 2018

The Russian Cosmonaut for the Russian Federal Space Agency, Oleg Artemyev, recently came back to Earth after working at the International Space Station from March to October, 2018. He has spent over 365 days in space in total, and has his own vlog to document life on the International Space Station. Oleg lives in Riga, Latvia.

Serena Auñón-Chancellor

Currently in space!

Serena is an American physician, engineer, and NASA astronaut, who's been in space since June 8th, 2018, serving as a flight engeneer. Well educated she holds a bachelor of science degree in Elecrical Engineering, a doctor of medicine degree as well as a master of public health degree. NASA hired her as a flight surgeon and she has received an award for her crewmember clinical care and development of medical kits supporting launch and landing. This is Serena's first trip to space!

Sergey Prokopyev

Currently in space!

Sergey Prokopyev of the Russian Federal Space Agency is a flight engineer for Expedition 56 and 57. His rank is commander and finished his training in 2012. Sergey lives in Sverdlovsk, Russia, but is still in space, working at the International Space Station since June 2018. This is Sergey's first trip to space!

Richard Arnold

Arrived at Earth Oct. 2018

Richard is an American educator and NASA astronaut who stayed at the International Space Station from March to October 2018 on his most recent trip to space. Prior to becoming an astronaut he worked as an Oceanographic technician and taught Biology and Marine Environmental Science at college. Richard has lived a lot of places, including Marocco, Saudi Arabia and Romania.
